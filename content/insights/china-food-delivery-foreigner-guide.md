# China Food Delivery for Foreigners (2026): Meituan and Ele.me Without Headaches

Food delivery in China is fast and efficient, but first-time foreign users often fail at address precision, payment setup, and order notes.

## First-order checklist

- Payment apps already tested.
- Address copied in Chinese format.
- Building/entrance instructions prepared.
- Phone reachable for delivery call or message.

## Platform choice basics

- Use the app with better coverage in your district.
- Compare delivery fee + estimated time, not only food price.
- Keep both platforms as fallback during peak meal windows.

## Address strategy that avoids failed delivery

1. Use exact building and gate identifiers.
2. Add floor/room details in short format.
3. Include visible landmark in notes.

## Ordering workflow

1. Pick restaurant with clear reviews and realistic prep time.
2. Confirm portion and spice level details.
3. Add delivery notes with concise Chinese + English if possible.
4. Track rider location and be reachable near arrival window.

## Common foreigner pain points

### 1. Wrong location pin
Recheck map pin before placing order.

### 2. Payment timeout
Switch to secondary payment path quickly.

### 3. Driver cannot find entrance
Use landmark + gate number + short call/message template.

### 4. Food mismatch
Keep screenshot of order summary for support flow.

## Safety and quality filters

- Prefer restaurants with strong consistency signals.
- Avoid extreme prep-time promises when quality matters.
- During weather spikes, add delivery buffer.

## Final recommendation

Food delivery works smoothly once your address and payment layers are stable. Build that foundation once and reuse it every day.
